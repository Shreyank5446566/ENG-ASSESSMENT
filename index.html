Tawang





Off beat destination Tawang-

Tawang, a picturesque town in Arunachal Pradesh, is famous for its majestic landscapes, Buddhist monasteries, and rich cultural heritage. Tawang offers a captivating blend of natural beauty and historical sites, making it a must-visit destination for travellers. Here are some of the best places to visit in Tawang

why is it off beat-

  • Unlike popular tourist destinations, Tawang has limited infrastructure catering to tourists. There are fewer hotels, restaurants, and amenities
  • Tawang is located in a far-flung corner of India, in the state of Arunachal Pradesh. The journey to Tawang involves long drives through mountainous terrain and crossing high-altitude passes like the Sela Pass. The difficulty in accessing Tawang makes it less visited.

Why should we visit Tawang?

  • Tibetan Buddhist Heritage: Tawang is a significant center for Tibetan Buddhism. The Tawang Monastery, one of the largest and oldest monasteries in India.
  • Tawang is nestled in the Eastern Himalayas, offering breathtaking views of snow-capped peaks, lush valleys, and pristine lakes.
  • The area is home to diverse flora and fauna, including rare and endangered species. The region's high-altitude environment supports unique ecosystems.
  • Tawang is known for its traditional Tibetan crafts, including intricate hand-woven carpets, traditional clothing, and Buddhist artifacts. The Tawang Craft Centre showcases these local handicrafts.

Places to visit in Tawang-

  1. Tawang Monastery
  2. Jaswant Garh-

    3. A war memorial dedicated to Jaswant Singh Rawat, a soldier of the Indian Army who made significant sacrifices during the 1962 Sino-Indian War.

  3. Sela Pass-

    4. A high-altitude mountain pass located at an elevation of about 4,170 meters. The pass offers breathtaking views of the snow-capped peaks and is often surrounded by pristine snow during the winter months.

  4. Madhuri Lake
  5. Tawang War Memorial

    6. A memorial dedicated to the soldiers who lost their lives in the 1962 war. It has a museum with historical displays and a monument with a sacred flame.

  6. Nuranang Falls (Jang Falls)
  7. Tawang Craft Centre-

    8. A place where you can see and purchase traditional handicrafts and souvenirs made by local artisans.

  8. Taktsang Gompa (Tiger’s Nest)

    9. A monastery located on a hilltop offering panoramic views of the surrounding valleys and mountains. It’s a bit of a trek but worth the effort for the views and experience.


Culture of Tawang-

People & Ethnic Groups

  • Monpa Tribe
  • Other Tribes: Sherdukpen, Miji, and Takpa
  • Language: Monpa Dialects, Tibetan, Hindi, English

Religion-

  • Predominantly Tibetan Buddhism
    • Gelugpa Sect of Buddhism
    • Tawang Monastery (largest monastery in India)

Festivals-

  • Losar
    • Traditional dances (Cham dances)
    • Offerings and prayers for prosperity
  • Torgya
    • Monastic dance festivals
    • Religious ceremonies for peace and protection
  • Dukpa Tse-She
    • Commemoration of Buddha's first teaching

Music & Dance-

  • Bumchu (traditional Monpa music)
  • Cham Dance (masked dance performed by monks during festivals)
  • Folk Songs (celebrating nature and spiritual life)

Food / lodging

Food-

  • The Dragon Restaurant Cuisine: Tibetan and Chinese Highlights: Known for its authentic Tibetan dishes, including momos (dumplings) and thukpa (noodle soup). The restaurant provides a cozy atmosphere with warm service.
  • Osmans Restaurant Cuisine: Indian, Chinese, and Tibetan Highlights: Offers a diverse menu with a mix of Indian, Chinese, and Tibetan dishes. It’s a popular choice for travelers looking for variety and quality.
  • Rai’s Restaurant Cuisine: Tibetan and Chinese Highlights: Known for its flavorful Tibetan cuisine and relaxing ambiance. It’s a great spot to enjoy local specialties.
  • Yarlung Restaurant Cuisine: Tibetan and Chinese Highlights: Provides a range of traditional Tibetan dishes along with popular Chinese options. It’s a great place to try local flavors
